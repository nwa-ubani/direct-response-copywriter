01 · Sales Letter — WealthGuard™ (Financial Newsletter)

Objective: acquire cold subscribers from native ads and email swaps at target CPA while building trust in a skeptical market.

Context & Audience

Market: Personal finance | Ticket: $79/yr | Stage: Cold | Goal: List growth (CPA ≤ $28)

Audience: 35–62, mid‑career, anxious about inflation & retirement. Previously burned by hypey “get‑rich” claims.

Challenges & Gaps
  • Low trust: fear of scams; compliance constraints limit promises.
  • Generic hooks (“market insights”) underperform; no quantified outcomes.
  • No risk reversal; weak proof stack; vague CTAs.
Strategy

Use PAS → Story → Mechanism → Proof → Offer → Risk Reversal. Anchor on an economic survival mechanism vs. stock picks. Normalize fear, then present plan.

AuthorityLoss aversionSpecificityGuarantee
Message Architecture
  • Big Idea: “Your paycheck didn’t shrink — your money did.”
  • Promise: Protect purchasing power in 90 days with a 3‑layer plan.
  • Mechanism: The “Inflation Shield” (cashflow, hedges, timing).
  • Proof: 12‑year audited track of model portfolios; subscriber outcomes.
  • Offer: $79/yr + bonus guides + 60‑day guarantee.
Before (open): “Subscribe for weekly market insights.”
Gap: Abstract; no pain, no mechanism, no reason to act now.
Sales Letter — Opening (Excerpt)
If you’re worried your money won’t last another decade, you’re not alone. Inflation doesn’t announce itself — it just quietly steals 8%, then 6%, then 4%… until your retirement math falls apart. This letter shows how thousands of readers used one simple framework to protect purchasing power in 90 days — without gambling on risky trades.
Pain future pacingCredibilityMechanism
Experiments
  • H1 Test: Outcome vs. curiosity → +18% LP CVR for outcome (“Protect purchasing power in 90 days”).
  • CTA Copy: “Start Now” vs “See the 3‑Layer Plan” → +11% CTR.
  • Guarantee: 30 vs 60 days → -9% refunds, +6% sales.
Results & Learnings

CPA $24.70 (target ≤ $28), Take‑rate +37%, Refunds 4.6%.

Wins came from specificity + mechanism naming. Compliance‑friendly proof (audits, “model” language) preserved trust without hype.

02 · Landing Page — SkinFix™ (Skincare Trial)

Objective: convert paid social traffic to a $9 trial kit; reduce drop‑off on mobile.

Context & Audience

Market: DTC skincare | AOV: $49 | Goal: Trial CVR ≥ 4.5%

Audience: 18–34, acne‑prone, tried 3–5 products in last year, ad‑fatigued.

Challenges & Gaps
  • Hero copy focused on ingredients, not outcomes.
  • Proof buried; mobile load slow; form overwhelm.
  • No time‑bound incentive; vague returns policy.
Before Hero: “Clinically tested niacinamide + HA formula.”
Gap: Ingredient worship; no “why now”; feels like a brochure.
New Hero (Mobile‑first)
Headline: Clearer skin in 14 days — or your money back. Sub: 3‑step kit matched to your skin in 60 seconds. CTA: Start my 14‑day trial → Trust row: ★★★★★ 4.8 (12,431) · Dermatologist‑reviewed · 30‑day guarantee
OutcomeRisk reversalSpeedSocial proof
Experiments
  • CTA wording test (trial vs discount) → +22% CTR for “Start my trial”.
  • Progressive form (quiz first) → -31% drop‑off.
  • UGC near price vs below fold → +17% CVR.
Results & Learnings

CVR 5.1% (from 2.2%), Mobile speed +38%, Refunds stable.

Outcome + guarantee trumped ingredient‑first messaging. Lightweight quiz increased commitment before price reveal.

03 · Email Onboarding — SaaSFlow™ (B2B SaaS)

Objective: increase activation within 14 days and reduce early churn.

Context & Audience

ICP: 10–50‑person teams | Motion: PLG + sales assist | North‑star: first project created

Challenges & Gaps
  • Asking for payment method before value moment → friction.
  • Reminder‑only emails; no outcome proof.
  • No branching based on in‑app behavior.
3‑Email Micro‑Sequence (48h)
E1 — Why Now (Day 0) Subj: Still juggling tasks in spreadsheets? Body: "12,431 teams cut admin time by 42% last month. Create your first board in 60s →" E2 — What If (Day 1) Subj: What your week looks like after setup Body: GIF of before/after, 3 bullets: visibility, handoffs, deadlines. CTA: copy template. E3 — Offer (Day 2) Subj: Finish setup — get your first automation free Body: Risk reversal + 1‑click import. CTA: Finish setup.
Social proofFuture pacingLow‑effort CTA
Experiments
  • Subject line variants (problem vs outcome) → +9% OR, +14% CTR.
  • Branching by event (created project? yes/no) → +18% activation.
  • Deferring card ask until value moment → -23% early churn.
Results & Learnings

Activation +18%, Churn -12% at day‑30 cohort.

Embedding proof and sequencing to the value event beat reminder‑only nudges.
